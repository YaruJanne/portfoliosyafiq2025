About me

I'm a passionate and versatile game developer with a strong foundation in game design, programming, project management, and technical research. I specialize in Unreal Engine, with Unity as a secondary tool, and have led multiple creative and technical projects—ranging from games to cultural and university initiatives. .

As a game designer, I thrive on crafting engaging concepts and mechanics that offer fresh, meaningful experiences. As a lead programmer, I’ve driven the technical development of various titles, including complex systems and gameplay features. My recent work includes a Rhythm Boxing game integrated with Sony Mocopi, and a local co-op AI-themed story puzzle game.

In addition to development, I’m experienced in managing cross-disciplinary teams and delivering results across diverse project types. My final year project focused on creating a music game framework that automates beat cueing—designed to streamline content creation for future developers.

Fragmented Alliance
Overview

Fragmented Alliance is a narrative-driven strategy game developed for Workshop II at UTeM, exploring themes of trust, betrayal, and unity in a fractured world. Players engage in tactical decision-making, navigating shifting alliances and moral dilemmas that impact multiple factions. The game features a local co-op experience, allowing two players to collaborate or compete in strategic missions, encouraging discussion and joint decision-making that influences the storyline and outcomes. Awarded 1st Place in Poster Design, the project was recognized for its compelling visual presentation, thematic depth, and clear communication of game systems..

Platform

PC, Controller

Tools Used

Unreal, After Effect, Media Encoding, Audacity

Development Challenge

Creating fair and engaging tactical encounters for both cooperative and competitive modes involved extensive playtesting. We had to constantly adjust difficulty scaling, enemy AI behavior, and turn-based mechanics to maintain balance and tension.

Only Forward
Description

Only Forward is a game focus on simplicity yet deceptive mechanism, featuring randomness event such as dark sky, perspective change and other everytime the player died. Having time ticking making the player rush that effect player decision on progressing the level. The game also featured in side scrolling style which confuse the player after dying as it change the perspective of the player. Enemies are introduce to block player movement and ensure player get punished on clumsy run.

Tools Used
